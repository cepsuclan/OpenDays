[I] Why Cybersecurity Matters to You

You might not be training to be a security professional, but in today's digital world, every role is a cybersecurity role. Whether you end up in finance, management, engineering, or marketing, you will be handling valuable data. Understanding the fundamentals of digital risk, data breaches, and incident response is no longer optionalâ€”it's a core professional skill. This site is designed to give you that hands-on, non-technical experience.

Think of yourself as a Digital Detective. We won't focus on writing code, but on using logic and analysis to understand the narrative of a breach: who, what, where, and how.

[II] Your Training Pathway

We will progress through three stages, each building on the last, moving you from passive observer to active decision-maker in a crisis scenario.

STAGE 1: Level 1 Incident (The Simple Path)

We begin with a Level 1 Incident, which represents a common, easily traceable attack. Your goal is to analyze provided log data to identify the attacker's Initial Access Point, their Lateral Movement, and their Final Objective. This is about finding the clear, simple path of a basic breach.

STAGE 2: Level 2 Incident (The Complex Attack)

Next, you will tackle a Level 2 Incident. The attacker's techniques here are more sophisticated, requiring closer examination and a deeper understanding of privilege escalation and stealth. The data set will be more complex, and the compromise will be less obvious.

STAGE 3: Breach Simulator (Consequence & Choice)

Finally, you will enter the Breach Simulator. Here, another incident is active, and the outcome is determined by the critical choices you make as the Incident Commander. Your decisions on containment, eradication, and communication will have immediate, tangible consequences on the organization's recovery and reputation.
